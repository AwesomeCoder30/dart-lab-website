# Page Specs

## Home

### Hero

- full-width institutional presentation
- restrained background treatment
- acceptable option: Congress or similar civic/government imagery at low opacity
- no distracting video unless execution is unusually clean
- headline should explain what DART Lab is in plain language
- supporting copy should position the organization as a policy and research institution
- primary actions should point to `Publications`, `Programs`, and `Get Involved`

### By the Numbers

- compact metrics section inspired by GHSEN
- use 3-4 metrics max
- metrics should be large, clear, and supported by short labels

Suggested starter metrics:

- publications produced
- students involved
- institutions represented
- mentors / collaborators

### What We Do

Three blocks:

1. Research
2. Policy Briefs / Op-eds
3. Volunteering

Each block should have a short description and a path deeper into the relevant page.

### Featured Work

- surface a small number of strong outputs
- each item should show title, short summary, date, and authors
- avoid overcrowding

### Who We Work With

- institution or partner logo band
- grayscale or restrained treatment preferred
- use only real logos with approval

### Primary CTA Band

- clear next steps
- emphasize `Publications`, `Programs`, and `Get Involved`

## Publications

- one of the highest-priority pages on the site
- should feel polished and easy to scan
- inspired by Brookings research/commentary structure
- every item should include:
  - title
  - summary
  - author names
  - publication date
  - category
  - PDF or external link
- should be backed by a CMS collection
- should allow future growth without redesign

## Programs

Programs should not read like one long generic page. Break the content into four clearly separated areas:

1. Policy Brief Program
2. Research
3. Volunteering Program
4. Op-eds

Each program area should include:

- short overview
- who it is for
- what the work looks like
- expected outputs or outcomes
- call to action where relevant

## Team

### Sections

1. Leadership
2. Advisors
3. Collaborators
4. Members
5. Alumni

### Card Requirements

Every visible card should include:

- name
- school and year, for example `Cornell '26`

Leadership cards should also include:

- title / position

### Expanded Profile

On click, each person should open:

- a dedicated profile page, or
- a modal with expanded content

Expanded content should support:

- fuller bio
- LinkedIn
- email
- headshot
- relevant external links

### Visibility Pattern

For `Collaborators`, `Members`, and `Alumni`:

- show top 4-5 by default
- expose the remainder through a `See More` reveal or equivalent clean expansion pattern

### Leadership Starter Roles

Leadership should include:

- founder / lead
- Grace
- Kevin
- Thansi
- one future placeholder role

## About

This page should explain the organization more deeply than the homepage.

### Who We Are

- mission
- concise description of the organization
- founder story / background

### How DART Lab Works

- how students, mentors, collaborators, and projects fit together
- how outputs are produced
- how the organization operates in practice

### Who We Work With

- partnerships
- collaborators
- institutional support
- external relationships

The page should make the organization feel thoughtfully built and well-run.

## Get Involved

### For Professionals

- Mentor
- Partner

### For Students

- Research
- Volunteering
- Writing

This page should help different audiences self-select quickly.

## Contact

Keep this page simple.

Include:

- email
- phone if needed
- embedded Google Form
